Investor Education

Build your foundation

Everything you need to know before your first trade — explained simply.

What is a stock?

Buying a stock means buying a small piece of ownership in a company. If the company grows and earns more profit, your piece becomes worth more. If it struggles, it loses value.

Like owning a slice of a pizza shop. If the shop gets popular, your slice is worth more.

RSI — Momentum

Relative Strength Index measures buying/selling momentum on a scale of 0–100. It tells you whether a stock is being overbought (too much buying) or oversold (too much selling).

RSI above 70 = possibly overbought. Below 30 = possibly oversold. These are signals, not guarantees.

Moving Averages

SMA smooths out daily price noise to show the real trend. The 20-day shows short-term momentum, the 50-day shows the medium-term direction traders follow.

Price crossing above SMA 50 = often bullish. Crossing below = often bearish.

Diversification

Never put all your money in one stock. Spread across companies, sectors, and even countries. If one company fails, your whole portfolio doesn't go with it.

A single ETF like VOO gives you 500 companies at once — instant diversification for beginners.

⏳ The power of starting early — Compound Interest

Your investment returns earn more returns over time. The earlier you start, the more powerful this effect becomes. Below: $1,000 invested at 8% annual return — starting at age 16 vs 26.

Essential terms — hover any for a plain English explanation

P/E RATIO
Price divided by earnings per share. The market's grade on how expensive a stock is. Average is ~20-25x.
MARKET CAP
Total value of all shares. Small cap <$2B, Mid $2-10B, Large >$10B, Mega >$200B.
BETA
How much the stock moves vs the S&P 500. Beta 2.0 = moves twice as wildly as the market.
EPS
Earnings Per Share — profit per share of stock. Higher = company is making more money per share.
DIVIDEND
Cash paid to you just for holding the stock — like rental income from owning property.
52-WEEK RANGE
Highest and lowest price over the past year. Wide range = volatile stock.
BULL MARKET
Prices rising 20%+ from a recent low. Optimism is high. Historically lasts longer than bear markets.
BEAR MARKET
Prices falling 20%+ from a recent high. Fear is high. Always temporary in history.
