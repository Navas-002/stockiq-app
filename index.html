Investor Education

Build your foundation

Everything you need before your first trade.

📈 What is a stock?

Buying a stock means buying a small piece of ownership in a company. If the company grows, your piece becomes worth more. If it struggles, it can lose value.

💡 Like owning a slice of a pizza shop — if it gets popular, your slice is worth more.

📊 RSI — Momentum

Relative Strength Index measures how fast prices are moving, on a scale of 0–100. Tells you whether a stock is being overbought or oversold.

💡 Above 70 = possibly overbought. Below 30 = possibly oversold. Signals, not certainties.

📉 Moving Averages

SMA smooths out daily price noise to show the real trend. The 20-day shows short-term momentum. The 50-day shows the medium-term direction.

💡 Price above SMA 50 = uptrend. Below it = downtrend.

🥚 Diversification

Spreading your money across different companies and sectors reduces risk. If one stock crashes, your whole portfolio doesn't go with it.

💡 A single index fund like VOO gives you 500 companies at once — instant diversification.

⏳ Why starting early matters

Compound interest means your returns earn more returns. Starting at 16 vs 26 can mean hundreds of thousands of dollars difference by retirement — same $1,000 invested, just more time.

📖 Key terms

P/E RATIO
Price vs earnings per share. Lower = cheaper, higher = more growth expected. Average ~20-25x.
MARKET CAP
Total value of all shares. Small <$2B, Mid $2-10B, Large >$10B.
BETA
How much the stock moves vs the market. Beta 2 = moves twice as much as the S&P 500.
EPS
Earnings Per Share — profit the company makes per share of stock outstanding.
DIVIDEND
Cash paid to you just for holding the stock — like rent income from owning property.
52-WEEK RANGE
Highest and lowest price over the past year — shows how volatile the stock is.
BULL MARKET
Prices rising 20%+ from a recent low. Optimism is high. Good time to be invested.
BEAR MARKET
Prices falling 20%+ from a recent high. Fear is high. Historically always temporary.
