Investor Education

Build your foundation

Everything you need to know before your first trade.

📈 What is a stock?

Buying a stock means buying a small piece of ownership in a company. If the company grows and profits, your piece becomes worth more. If it struggles, it can lose value.

💡 Like owning a slice of a business — if the business does well, so do you.

📊 RSI Explained

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) measures buying and selling momentum on a scale of 0–100. It tells you if a stock is being overbought or oversold.

💡 Above 70 = possibly overbought. Below 30 = possibly oversold. These are signals, not certainties.

📉 Moving Averages

SMA smooths out price data over time. The 20-day shows short-term trend. The 50-day shows medium-term. When price crosses above, it can signal momentum.

💡 Price above SMA 50 = uptrend. Below it = downtrend.

🥚 Diversification

Never put all your money in one stock. Spreading across different companies and sectors reduces your risk. One company failing won't wipe you out.

💡 A single ETF like VOO gives you instant exposure to 500 companies at once.

📖 Key terms

BULL MARKET
Prices rising 20%+ from a recent low — optimism is high
BEAR MARKET
Prices falling 20%+ from a recent high — pessimism is high
DIVIDEND
Cash a company pays you just for holding their stock
VOLATILITY
How wildly a stock's price swings up and down
ETF
A basket of many stocks you can buy as a single share
MARKET CAP
Total value of all a company's outstanding shares
P/E RATIO
Stock price divided by earnings — higher means more expensive
INDEX FUND
Tracks a market index like S&P 500 — low cost, diversified
